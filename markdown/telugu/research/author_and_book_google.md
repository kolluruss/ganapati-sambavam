The author of the Ganapati Sambhavam (Gaṇapati-sambhavam) Sanskrit Mahakavya is Mahamahopadhyaya Vidya-Vachaspati Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri. [1] 
While he shares the name "Prabhudatta Shastri" with the Oxford-educated professor who wrote The Doctrine of Maya, the author of this specific epic poem was a distinct, traditional 20th-century Sanskrit poet and scholar (living from 1892 to 1979). His Ganapati Sambhavam Mahakavya is a highly regarded modern epic written in classical Sanskrit meters following traditional rules (Gana rules). It was published in 1968 by Archana Prakashan in Nagpur. [1, 2]

## Literary Themes of Ganapati Sambhavam
The title Ganapati Sambhavam translates to "The Birth of Ganapati," intentionally echoing Kalidasa’s classical masterpiece, Kumārasambhavam (The Birth of the War-God Kumara/Kartikeya). Written in a classical Mahakavya (epic poem) framework, Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri's work explores several major structural and philosophical themes: [1, 2, 3, 4] 

* The Divine Manifestation: The core narrative centers on the origins and birth of Lord Ganesha from the divine union and energies of Shiva and Parvati. It explores his theological role as Vighnaharta (the remover of obstacles). [5, 6, 7, 8] 
* Devotion and Bhakti Rasa: While traditional epic elements (Vira or heroic rasa) are present, the poem is deeply saturated with Bhakti (devotion). It emphasizes Ganesha as the personification of cosmic wisdom, intellect, and primary worship. [7, 9, 10] 
* Integration of Puranic Myth: Shastri weaves traditional Puranic accounts (such as Ganesha's creation by Parvati and the eventual replacement of his head with an elephant head) into strict, elegant Sanskrit meters. The themes blend cosmic allegory with accessible spiritual lessons. [5, 7, 9] 

## How to Find the Book
Because it is a mid-20th-century work published by a regional press, it can be slightly difficult to find in standard commercial bookstores, but there are a few definitive avenues:

* Libraries: The original 1968 edition published by Archana Prakashan (Nagpur) is indexed on [WorldCat](https://www.worldcat.org/title/ganapati-sambhavam/oclc/21005521). You can use this listing to check if any major university libraries or oriental research institutes holding Sanskrit manuscripts have a copy near you. [3] 
* Critical Studies: If you read Hindi and want an analytical breakdown of the epic poem, look for the book Ganapati Sambhavam Mahakavya Ka Sameekshaatmak Adhyayan (A Critical Study of Ganapati Sambhavam) by Hukam Singh. It features the Sanskrit text alongside detailed Hindi translations and commentary. It is occasionally kept in stock by specialized Indian book distributors like [Exotic India Art](https://www.exoticindiaart.com/book/details/ganapati-sambhavam-mahaakavya-ka-sameekshaatmak-adhyayan-tzz096/).
