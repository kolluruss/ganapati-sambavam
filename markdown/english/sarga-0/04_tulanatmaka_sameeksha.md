# A Comparative Study with Kalidasa's Kumarasambhavam

In Sanskrit literature, the Mahakavya 'Kumarasambhavam', composed by the great poet Kalidasa, occupies a place without parallel. Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri's modern Mahakavya 'Ganapati Sambhavam' is a wondrous continuation of that ancient poetic tradition. When these two Mahakavyas are examined comparatively, with a scholar's eye, one clearly sees both the episodes in which Shastri-ji follows in Kalidasa's footsteps, and the variations in which he weaves in his own independent, modern, and secular ideas.

## Episodes Inspired by Kalidasa

### The Kavya's Invocation — Description of the Himalayas

It is notable that both Mahakavyas begin with a description of the Himalaya mountain.

Where Kalidasa describes the Himalayas as the very soul of the gods, a measuring-rod for the earth —

**अस्त्युत्तरस्यां दिशि देवतात्मा हिमालयो नाम नगाधिराजः ।**
*astyuttarasyāṃ diśi devatātmā himālayo nāma nagādhirājaḥ |*

**पूर्वापरौ तोयनिधी वगाह्य स्थितः पृथिव्या इव मानदंडः ॥**
*pūrvāparau toyanidhī vagāhya sthitaḥ pṛthivyā iva mānadaṃḍaḥ ||*

— Kumarasambhavam

Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri describes the Himalayas as an aged Indian elder, wearing a white turban, standing guard over the northern direction —

**अस्त्यत्रोत्तरदिक्-सुरक्षणचणः, शैलो हिमानी-धनी**
*astyatrottaradik-surakṣaṇacaṇaḥ, śailo himānī-dhanī*

**श्वेतोष्णीषधरेषु भारतवयोवृद्धेषु मन्येऽग्रणीः ।**
*śvetoṣṇīṣadhareṣu bhāratavayovṛddheṣu manye'graṇīḥ |*

**व्यक्तोऽस्येव किरीटवत् 'स्फटिक' इत्याख्यः सितांशुर्मणिः**
*vyakto'syeva kirīṭavat 'sphaṭika' ityākhyaḥ sitāṃśurmaṇiḥ*

**तेनैवाऽस्य यशस्ततान भुवनेऽभूत् कालिदासोऽनृणी ॥**
*tenaivā'sya yaśastatāna bhuvane'bhūt kālidāso'nṛṇī ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

### The Birth of Parvati

The birth of Jagadamba Parvati Devi in the womb of Menaka, wife of the Himalayas, is described most beautifully in both Mahakavyas. Shastri-ji, too, clearly states that the same goddess who had been Sati Devi in her former birth was reborn as Menaka's daughter.

**प्रसन्नदिक्पांसुविविक्तवातं शंखस्वनानंतरपुष्पवृष्टि ।**
*prasannadikpāṃsuviviktavātaṃ śaṃkhasvanānaṃtarapuṣpavṛṣṭi |*

**शरीरिणां स्थावरजंगमानां सुखाय तज्जन्मदिनं बभूव ॥**
*śarīriṇāṃ sthāvarajaṃgamānāṃ sukhāya tajjanmadinaṃ babhūva ||*

— Kumarasambhavam

**'मेनैणी' नयनी स्व-कुक्षि पटलात् कन्याऽऽख्यरत्नं ददे ।**
*'menaiṇī' nayanī sva-kukṣi paṭalāt kanyā''khyaratnaṃ dade |*

**सौभाग्याभिध रत्नदान निपुणं कन्यांतरेभ्योऽपि यत् ॥**
*saubhāgyābhidha ratnadāna nipuṇaṃ kanyāṃtarebhyo'pi yat ||*

**आसीद् दक्षसुता स्वपूर्व जनने ख्याता 'सती' नामतः ।**
*āsīd dakṣasutā svapūrva janane khyātā 'satī' nāmataḥ |*

**सैवाऽद्यावि रभूद् गिरींद्रसदने ज्योतिस्तति र्जंगमा ॥**
*saivā'dyāvi rabhūd girīṃdrasadane jyotistati rjaṃgamā ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

### The Meaning of the Name 'Aparna'

The poet has drawn directly from the Kumarasambhavam the episode of Parvati Devi's severe austerity — undertaken to win Parameshvara Shiva as her husband — in which she abstained even from eating leaves, and thereby earned the name 'Aparna' (she who ate not even a leaf).

**स्वयं विशीर्णद्रुमपर्णवृत्तिता परा हि काष्ठा तपसस्तया पुनः ।**
*svayaṃ viśīrṇadrumaparṇavṛttitā parā hi kāṣṭhā tapasastayā punaḥ |*

**तदप्यपाकीर्णमतः प्रियंवदां अपर्णेति च तां पुराविदः ॥**
*tadapyapākīrṇamataḥ priyaṃvadāṃ aparṇeti ca tāṃ purāvidaḥ ||*

— Kumarasambhavam

**लब्धुं या शिव माद नैव तपसि प्राप्तं च पर्णं तरोः ।**
*labdhuṃ yā śiva māda naiva tapasi prāptaṃ ca parṇaṃ taroḥ |*

**कर्णार्कार्ण समेतसद्‌गुणचयं चंद्रावतंसाऽह्वयम् ॥**
*karṇārkārṇa sametasad‌guṇacayaṃ caṃdrāvataṃsā'hvayam ||*

**तेनापीह मतेव या यतिवरै वृक्षादिपत्राऽऽशनैः ।**
*tenāpīha mateva yā yativarai vṛkṣādipatrā''śanaiḥ |*

**अभ्यर्णस्थसुवर्ण लज्जनकरी क्रांत्याख्यकांत्या युता ॥**
*abhyarṇasthasuvarṇa lajjanakarī krāṃtyākhyakāṃtyā yutā ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

### Parameshvara Shiva's Yogic Samadhi

Shiva entering into deep meditation upon the Himalayan peaks, fixing his gaze between the eyebrows, and practicing pranayama, is drawn entirely from Kalidasa's descriptions. Shastri-ji has wondrously described the hearing of the 'So'ham' sound within the breath.

**पर्यंकबंधस्थिरपूर्वकायं ऋज्वायतं सन्नमितोभयांसम् ।**
*paryaṃkabaṃdhasthirapūrvakāyaṃ ṛjvāyataṃ sannamitobhayāṃsam |*

**उत्तानपाणिद्वयसन्निवेशात् प्रफुल्लराजीवमिवांकमध्ये ॥**
*uttānapāṇidvayasanniveśāt praphullarājīvamivāṃkamadhye ||*

— Kumarasambhavam

**प्राणयामपरै रवैक्षि विमलो 'हं' 'सो' निजश्वासयोः ।**
*prāṇayāmaparai ravaikṣi vimalo 'haṃ' 'so' nijaśvāsayoḥ |*

**आगच्छत्प्रतिगच्छतो र्ध्वनिकरो निश्चंचुपत्पक्षकः ॥**
*āgacchatpratigacchato rdhvanikaro niścaṃcupatpakṣakaḥ ||*

**ये चिन्वंति सुमौक्तिकेन सदृशान् श्वासान् प्रकाशान्वितान् ।**
*ye cinvaṃti sumauktikena sadṛśān śvāsān prakāśānvitān |*

**ते हंसाः परमाः सुचिन्मयसरस्वत्याः प्रिया वाहनाः ॥**
*te haṃsāḥ paramāḥ sucinmayasarasvatyāḥ priyā vāhanāḥ ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

### The Great Festival of the Bestowal of the Bride

Just as the Kumarasambhavam describes the bride and groom joining hands, so too Pandit Prabhudatta-ji, in the episode of the giving away of the bride (Kanyadana), most naturally likens a daughter's passage from her father's lineage to her husband's lineage to the river Ganga leaving her place of birth to reach the ocean.

**तस्याः करं वड्रमुखो मुखेन व्याकंपमानांगुलिमाजघाम । …**
*tasyāḥ karaṃ vaḍramukho mukhena vyākaṃpamānāṃgulimājaghāma | …*

— Kumarasambhavam

**तस्मादस्य करेऽर्ण्यतां जलमिदं पत्यैव संबंधकृत् ।**
*tasmādasya kare'rṇyatāṃ jalamidaṃ patyaiva saṃbaṃdhakṛt |*

**गंगायाःसलिलं यथोद्गमभुवं त्यक्त्वैति भूम्यंतरम् ॥**
*gaṃgāyāḥsalilaṃ yathodgamabhuvaṃ tyaktvaiti bhūmyaṃtaram ||*

**गंगा शीर्षधृता करोति शपथाचारान् दृढान् सन्नृणाम् ।**
*gaṃgā śīrṣadhṛtā karoti śapathācārān dṛḍhān sannṛṇām |*

**साऽप्येतेन धृताऽधुना तदभयत् पाणिग्रहोऽयं दृढः ॥**
*sā'pyetena dhṛtā'dhunā tadabhayat pāṇigraho'yaṃ dṛḍhaḥ ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

## Novel Departures in the Ganapati Sambhavam

While honoring the ancient poetic tradition, Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri wove in contemporary social, political, and national realities, creating a new style altogether distinct from Kalidasa's.

### Contemporary Geographical Descriptions (Kashmir, Nepal)

Where Kalidasa described only the mythic Himalaya, Shastri-ji describes Kashmir as the very chest of the Himalayas and Nepal as its head, in the most vivid and modern geographical terms. The poet notes that Kashmir, famed for its saffron, remains dear to us even though it has suffered the ravages of demonic raiders.

**काश्मीरेति पदाभिधं गिरिपते र्वक्षोऽक्षिलक्ष्यम् जनैः ।**
*kāśmīreti padābhidhaṃ giripate rvakṣo'kṣilakṣyam janaiḥ |*

**रक्षस्तक्षणभक्षणक्षय मितं किंचित् तथापि प्रियम् ॥**
*rakṣastakṣaṇabhakṣaṇakṣaya mitaṃ kiṃcit tathāpi priyam ||*

— Description of Kashmir, Ganapati Sambhavam

**नेपालोऽस्य गिरे र्ललाट तट-भः प्राशस्त्य-भूयस्त्ववान् ।**
*nepālo'sya gire rlalāṭa taṭa-bhaḥ prāśastya-bhūyastvavān |*

**कस्तूरी मृगबंधुरः प्रतिवनं घ्रातुंच मित्राणि यम् ॥**
*kastūrī mṛgabaṃdhuraḥ prativanaṃ ghrātuṃca mitrāṇi yam ||*

— Description of Nepal, Ganapati Sambhavam

### Modern Weapons of Defense (Tanks and Bombs)

The poet's bold and startling independent invention is his introduction, into this classical Mahakavya, of modern weapons unknown in Kalidasa's time — tanks, bombs, and guns — in his description of the Indian soldiers standing guard over the Himalayas.

**'वारूदं' च कृपीटयोनिजननं तद्-गोपकं 'बम्' च यत् ।**
*'vārūdaṃ' ca kṛpīṭayonijananaṃ tad-gopakaṃ 'bam' ca yat |*

**यत्-पार्श्वे शयनं करोति सखिवत् जागर्ति संकेततः ॥**
*yat-pārśve śayanaṃ karoti sakhivat jāgarti saṃketataḥ ||*

**"'टांकान्' वक्रमुखान् शशांकसदृशान् नूत्नान् नवांकांश्च वा ।**
*"'ṭāṃkān' vakramukhān śaśāṃkasadṛśān nūtnān navāṃkāṃśca vā |*

**कारंकार मिमे हसंति कपिवत् लंकाविटंकान् यथा ॥**
*kāraṃkāra mime hasaṃti kapivat laṃkāviṭaṃkān yathā ||*

— Bombs and Tanks, Ganapati Sambhavam

### The Creation of Ganapati from Clay through Yogic Power

Where Kumaraswami's birth proceeds through Shiva's radiance, by way of Agni, the Ganga, and the Krittikas, Ganapati is instead created by Parvati Devi from the natural residue of her own body, into which she breathes life through her yogic power — an entirely different method, which the poet defends not as mere Puranic legend but as a wondrous feat of yogic science.

**सा सम्मृद्य मृदं स्वशुद्धिरसिका कृत्वा च तद्धृतकाम् ।**
*sā sammṛdya mṛdaṃ svaśuddhirasikā kṛtvā ca taddhṛtakām |*

**ऐच्छ त्ता मपरत्र कुत्रचिदपि प्रक्षेप्तु मुद्यत्पदा ॥**
*aiccha ttā maparatra kutracidapi prakṣeptu mudyatpadā ||*

**संयोज्याऽपर्मृत्स्नया रज इदं कृत्वेव तत् पुष्कलम् ।**
*saṃyojyā'parmṛtsnayā raja idaṃ kṛtveva tat puṣkalam |*

**स्वाखं पुच्छकमुच्छतोऽतिललितं गिर्यात्मजा निर्ममे ॥**
*svākhaṃ pucchakamucchato'tilalitaṃ giryātmajā nirmame ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

### The Metaphor of Democratic Governance

The ninth sarga of this Mahakavya is the most revolutionary passage in the history of Sanskrit literature. The poet's comparison of Ganapati's very bodily form to modern democratic governance is a mark of his extraordinary vision. He defines the true 'Ganashasana' (rule of the Ganas) as governance that wins the goodwill of the people without mutual violence or corruption.

**यन्न स्याद् रणतंत्र मेव च मिथो मुष्टिप्रहारप्रियम् ।**
*yanna syād raṇataṃtra meva ca mitho muṣṭiprahārapriyam |*

**न स्यान्मुष्टिकरं समागतजनग्रंथिस्थवित्तस्य वा ॥**
*na syānmuṣṭikaraṃ samāgatajanagraṃthisthavittasya vā ||*

**चेतस्तुष्टिकरं सुपुष्टिकरणं स्नेहस्य देहस्य वा ।**
*cetastuṣṭikaraṃ supuṣṭikaraṇaṃ snehasya dehasya vā |*

**तत् प्रोक्तं गणशासनाऽपर पदं गाजाननं शासनम् ॥**
*tat proktaṃ gaṇaśāsanā'para padaṃ gājānanaṃ śāsanam ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

### Description of the National Flag

In the first sarga, in describing the Himalayas — red with flowers, green with trees, and white with snow — the poet observes that these mountain ranges themselves evoke India's national tricolor flag. This is a striking departure entirely absent from classical Sanskrit Mahakavyas, and stands as testament to the poet's boundless patriotism.

**पुष्पैःशोणतमो द्रुमैश्च हरितः श्वेत स्तुषारैश्च यः ।**
*puṣpaiḥśoṇatamo drumaiśca haritaḥ śveta stuṣāraiśca yaḥ |*

**नानाऽऽक्रामकवातघातकुशलोऽद्रिस्कंधसंबंधवान् ॥**
*nānā''krāmakavātaghātakuśalo'driskaṃdhasaṃbaṃdhavān ||*

**तोयांतःस्थ सरोजिनीमयमहामालादलाऽऽलिंगितः ।**
*toyāṃtaḥstha sarojinīmayamahāmālādalā''liṃgitaḥ |*

**हं हो !! भारत राष्ट्रसद्ध्वजविभा मालिंगती वांगतः ॥**
*haṃ ho !! bhārata rāṣṭrasaddhvajavibhā māliṃgatī vāṃgataḥ ||*

— Ganapati Sambhavam

---

If Kalidasa's 'Kumarasambhavam' stands as a sacred foundation for Sanskrit literature, then Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri's 'Ganapati Sambhavam' is the modern temple devotedly built atop that very foundation. Without abandoning classical poetic conventions, and while nourishing Kalidasa's own poetic flavors, Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri is a rare craftsman who shaped modern nationalist sentiment, the spirit of India's freedom struggle, and modern political thought into Sanskrit verse. There can be no doubt that this work is a golden bridge between the ancient and the modern.
