# Foreword

There is a sweet history behind bringing **'Ganapati Sambhavam'** — this wondrous Sanskrit Mahakavya composed by **Mahamahopadhyaya Pandit Prabhudatta Shastri**. My revered father, **Brahmasri Kolluru Avatara Sarma**, was a scholar of the Sanskrit language, a tireless seeker of knowledge, and a devoted man of letters who authored nearly 60 works on Sanatana Dharma. He was my Guru, and the root inspiration for this sacred undertaking. He was a great devotee of Ganesha who had himself translated the *'Mudgala Purana'* — dedicated specifically to the worship of Ganesha — into Telugu. While reading the *'Ganesha Upasanank'* published by Gita Press, he had noticed the humorous conversation between Ganapati and Parashurama quoted there from 'Ganapati Sambhavam', and he would often bring up those verses with us.

He searched for this rare work for many years. About two years ago, at his request, I searched for this text and learned that it was available at the 'Michigan State University Library' in America. Fortunately, my friend *Sri Palli Venkata Subbaraya Sarma* took great pains to procure this text through his son *Sattvik Sarma* and sent it to me.

After reading this work and coming to appreciate the immense literary treasures and wondrous episodes within it, my joy knew no bounds. This translated volume is my humble attempt, born of the earnest wish to offer this most sacred and singular Mahakavya, in our mother tongue of Telugu, to devotees of Ganesha and to connoisseurs who delight in Sanskrit literature.

This effort, undertaken for the joy of the soul (svāntaḥsukham) and for the welfare of the world, has employed artificial intelligence merely as a tool. I consider it to have served as a bridge between the vast treasury of knowledge left by the ancient masters and my own modest curiosity.

By the grace of Sri Ganesha, I hope this labor will be of some benefit to devotees. May all of this be an offering at the lotus feet of Sri Mahaganapati!

**Yours,**
**Kolluru Seetarama Sarma**
